Taking supplements is a great way to optimize your health, but throwing them all back in one handful can do more harm than good. Nutrients often interact with each other—some cancel each other out, while others work together to boost absorption.

Here is the ultimate guide to supplement interactions: what you should never mix, and what you should always pair up.

## 🔴 What NOT to Mix (The Clashing Combos)

**1. Iron and Calcium**
Calcium is notorious for blocking the absorption of iron. If you take an iron supplement for energy or anemia alongside a glass of milk, a calcium pill, or a multivitamin containing calcium, your body won't absorb the iron.
*Rule:* Keep them at least 2 hours apart.

**2. Magnesium and Zinc**
Both of these minerals are incredibly popular for sleep and immunity, but they compete for the same absorption pathways in your gut. Taking high doses of both at the exact same time means one will be left behind.
*Rule:* Take Zinc in the morning with food, and Magnesium at night before bed.

**3. Vitamin C and Vitamin B12**
High doses of Vitamin C can break down Vitamin B12 in your digestive tract, rendering the B12 useless.
*Rule:* Separate them by at least two hours.

**4. Zinc and Copper**
Taking high doses of Zinc for immunity can significantly deplete your body's Copper levels over time. Copper is essential for red blood cell formation and nervous system health.
*Rule:* If you take Zinc long-term, ensure you also take a small amount of Copper at a different time of day.

**5. Vitamin E and Vitamin K**
These two fat-soluble vitamins compete for absorption in the body. Taking high doses of Vitamin E can counteract the blood-clotting and bone-building effects of Vitamin K.
*Rule:* Separate them by several hours.

**6. Green Tea Extract (EGCG) and Iron**
Green tea extract is incredible for metabolism and antioxidants, but the EGCG compounds bind to iron and completely prevent its absorption.
*Rule:* Never take green tea supplements within 2 hours of an iron-rich supplement.

**7. St. John's Wort and Prescription Medications**
This popular herbal supplement for mood enhancement is a strong inducer of liver enzymes (specifically CYP3A4). This means it clears medications from your bloodstream too quickly.
*Rule:* It can render birth control pills, antidepressants, and heart medications ineffective. Always consult a doctor.

## 🟢 The Perfect Pairs (Synergistic Supplements)

**1. Vitamin D3 and Vitamin K2**
Vitamin D helps your body absorb calcium from your diet, but it doesn't know where to put it. Vitamin K2 acts as a traffic cop, directing that calcium directly into your bones and teeth instead of letting it build up in your arteries. Always take these together.

**2. Iron and Vitamin C**
While calcium blocks iron, Vitamin C is its best friend. Vitamin C creates an acidic environment in your gut that makes iron significantly easier to absorb. Take your iron pill with a glass of orange juice or a Vitamin C supplement.
